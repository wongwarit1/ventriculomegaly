About the Project

Ventriculomegaly is a condition characterized by the enlargement of the brainâ€™s lateral ventricles. It is one of the most common prenatal abnormalities detected via ultrasound, and its severity is categorized into mild, moderate, or severe based on ventricular width measurements. If left undiagnosed, it can potentially lead to neurological issues or developmental delays.
Example of Fetal Ventriculomegaly (for illustrative purposes only)

Encephalocele is a birth defect where the skull doesn't fully close during fetal development, causing a sac-like protrusion of brain tissue and membranes through the opening. Surgery is required to place the brain and membranes back in the skull.
Example of Encephalocele (for illustrative purposes only)

This system uses deep learning, specifically a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model, trained to classify ultrasound images into categories of ventriculomegaly severity. Users upload an image, and the model outputs a prediction along with a confidence score.

This software was developed by Wongwarit Jirapitchanun and his team, students from Srinakharinwirot University Demonstation School Prasanmit, under the supervision of Mister Weerarol Lapkoed.
